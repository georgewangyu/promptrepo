# Ray Dalio Principles: Ready-to-Use Prompts

## 1. Radical Transparency

**Core Prompt:**
```
If we're radically transparent, what's really true about [situation/decision/relationship]?
```

**Example:**
```
If we're radically transparent, what's really true about why my last three relationships failed?
```

**Variation (constructive delivery):**
```
If we're radically transparent, what's really true about [situation]? 
Help me reality-test this, not demolish my motivation.
```

**When to use:** When you suspect you're deceiving yourself, avoiding patterns, or need unvarnished truth.

---

## 2. Believability-Weighted Perspective

**Core Prompt:**
```
What's the believability-weighted perspective on [decision/situation]?
```

**Example:**
```
What's the believability-weighted perspective on starting a business in my 40s versus my 20s?
```

**With context:**
```
I'm considering [decision]. What's the believability-weighted perspective here?

Consider:
- People who've actually done this
- People who've failed at this
- Domain experts with track records
- What data says vs. what people say
```

**When to use:** When you have conflicting advice and need to weight it by evidence/track record, not confidence.

---

## 3. The Machine (Systems Thinking)

**Core Prompt:**
```
What's the machine behind [pattern/behavior/outcome]?
```

**Example:**
```
What's the machine behind why I keep procrastinating?
```

**Full analysis:**
```
Help me see the machine behind [recurring issue]:

1. What are the inputs to this system?
2. What are the outputs?
3. What feedback loops exist?
4. What cause-effect relationships am I missing?
5. If I wanted to design this outcome deliberately, how would I build the machine?
```

**When to use:** When you see a recurring pattern and want to understand the system causing it, not just the symptoms.

---

## 4. Second and Third-Order Consequences

**Core Prompt:**
```
What are the second and third-order consequences of [decision/action]?
```

**Example:**
```
I'm considering a job that pays 40% more but requires relocation. What are the second and third-order consequences?
```

**Structured analysis:**
```
I'm considering [decision]. Map the consequences:

First-order (direct result):
- 

Second-order (result of the result):
- 

Third-order (long-term ripple effects):
- 

What impacts on relationships, career trajectory, and lifestyle 5 years out am I not seeing?
```

**When to use:** When a decision has significant downstream effects you're not fully considering.

---

## 5. Principles for This Decision

**Core Prompt:**
```
What principles should govern this decision about [situation]?
```

**Example:**
```
I need to decide between two job offers. What principles should govern this decision?
```

**Full framework:**
```
Help me decide [between options / on situation].

1. What principles should govern this decision?
2. What are my actual values here (not what I think they should be)?
3. What would a decision I'm proud of in 10 years look like?
4. What principles would someone I admire apply here?
5. Based on these principles, which option aligns better?
```

**When to use:** When you want to build a consistent decision-making framework rather than one-off choices.

---

## 6. Idea Meritocracy (Who's Done It Better)

**Core Prompt:**
```
Who's handled [this problem] better than me and what would they do?
```

**Example:**
```
Who's handled career transitions better than me and what would they do in my situation?
```

**Expanded:**
```
For my challenge with [situation]:

1. Who has successfully solved this exact problem?
2. What specific actions did they take?
3. What would they do differently if they were me?
4. What mistakes did they make that I can avoid?
5. Synthesize their approach into specific steps for my situation.
```

**When to use:** When you need best practices from people who've actually solved your problem.

## 7. Pain + Reflection = Progress

**Core Prompt:**
```
Here's what went wrong: [situation]. 
What pain am I experiencing? What should I reflect on? What progress can I make?
```

**Example:**
```
I launched a product and got zero sales. What pain am I experiencing? What should I reflect on? What progress can I make?
```

**Systematic post-mortem:**
```
[Describe failure/painful outcome]

Apply Dalio's Pain + Reflection = Progress:

1. What specifically happened? (facts only)
2. What pain/discomfort am I feeling? (emotional check)
3. What principles can I extract from this?
4. How would I design the system/process differently?
5. What will I do differently next time?
6. What progress can I make from here?
```

**When to use:** After failures, setbacks, or painful outcomes — to convert pain into principles.

---

## 8. What Would Change My Mind?

**Core Prompt:**
```
What would change my mind about [belief/decision]?
```

**Example:**
```
I'm convinced I should [decision]. What would change my mind about this?
```

**Intellectual honesty test:**
```
On the question of [topic/decision]:

1. What evidence would actually shift my position?
2. What would I need to observe to prove myself wrong?
3. What biases might be preventing me from seeing contrary evidence?
4. What would a neutral third party conclude from the same data?
```

**When to use:** When you want to test if you're actually open to changing your mind, or just seeking confirmation.

---

## 9. Complete Decision Framework

**Use this as a comprehensive analysis:**

```
I need to make a decision about: [describe situation]

Apply Ray Dalio's complete framework:

1. RADICAL TRANSPARENCY: If we're radically transparent, what's really true about this situation that I might be avoiding?

2. THE MACHINE: What's the machine here — what cause-effect loops are driving this situation?

3. SECOND-ORDER THINKING: What are the second and third-order consequences of each option?

4. BELIEVABILITY-WEIGHTING: What's the believability-weighted perspective? What do people with track records in this domain say?

5. IDEA MERITOCRACY: Who's handled this better than me and what would they do?

6. PRINCIPLES: What principles should govern this decision? What aligns with my actual values?

7. REALITY-TEST: What would change my mind about each option? What evidence would prove one path wrong?

8. ACTION: Based on this analysis, what's the decision? What's the first step?
```

---

## Usage Tips

1. **Start with one** — Don't use all prompts at once. Pick the one that addresses your biggest blind spot.

2. **Be specific** — Replace `[situation]` with concrete details. Vague prompts get vague answers.

3. **Sit with discomfort** — These prompts are designed to surface uncomfortable truths. Don't dismiss them immediately.

4. **Build your own** — After using Dalio's principles, create your own: "Based on my last 10 major decisions, what principles do I actually operate by versus what I think I operate by?"

5. **Iterate** — Use the output as input for deeper questions. Follow the thread.
