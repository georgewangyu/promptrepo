# Ray Dalio's Principles: AI Prompts

Ray Dalio built Bridgewater Associates into the world's largest hedge fund using a system of "Principles" — radical transparency, idea meritocracy, and systematic decision-making.

These prompts translate Dalio's frameworks into AI instructions that give you a "brutally honest decision-making partner."

## The Core Idea

Dalio's central insight: **Pain + Reflection = Progress**

The discomfort of facing hard truths is the price of growth. These prompts force the uncomfortable truth AI won't sugarcoat.

## Key Principles

| Principle | What It Means | Prompt |
|-----------|---------------|--------|
| **Radical Transparency** | See things as they are, not as you wish them to be | "If we're radically transparent, what's really true about [situation]?" |
| **Believability-Weighting** | Weight advice by track record, not confidence | "What's the believability-weighted perspective on [decision]?" |
| **Idea Meritocracy** | Best ideas win, regardless of who has them | "Who's handled [problem] better than me and what would they do?" |
| **Second-Order Thinking** | Map downstream consequences | "What are the second and third-order consequences of [choice]?" |
| **The Machine** | See systems, not just events | "What's the machine behind [pattern/behavior]?" |
| **Pain as Signal** | Use painful outcomes as data | "What should I reflect on from [failure/pain]?" |

## When to Use These Prompts

- **Business decisions** — Pivots, investments, hiring, firing
- **Career choices** — Job changes, skill investments, paths
- **Relationship clarity** — When you need unvarnished truth about patterns
- **Personal habits** — Breaking self-deception about behaviors
- **Anytime you feel stuck** — Stuck often means you're avoiding a truth

## Warning

These prompts will tell you things like: *"Your business idea has been tried 50 times and failed for these specific reasons."*

Add `"help me reality-test this, not demolish my motivation"` if you need truth delivered constructively.
