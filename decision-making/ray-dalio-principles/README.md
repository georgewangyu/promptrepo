# Ray Dalio's Principles

A decision-making layer built from Ray Dalio's core ideas: radical transparency, believability-weighted thinking, second-order consequences, and principled reflection.

## Source

Adapted from Ray Dalio's published principles and the existing category notes in PromptRepo.

## Core Idea

Better decisions come from facing reality directly, weighting perspectives by actual track record, and treating pain or failure as input for better principles.

## Why It Works

### Radical Transparency

It strips away the comforting story and forces the real constraint into view.

### Believability-Weighted Thinking

It prevents "all opinions count the same" reasoning in areas where experience matters.

### Second-Order Thinking

It reduces shallow optimization by forcing you to think past the immediate effect.

### Pain + Reflection = Progress

It turns mistakes into operating principles rather than repeated confusion.

## When To Use It

- High-stakes personal decisions
- Career choices
- Business or investment tradeoffs
- Situations where emotion or ego is distorting the read
- Postmortems after painful outcomes

## Caveats

- Radical transparency can become performative or harsh if it is detached from actual problem-solving.
- Believability weighting is useful only when the domain fit is real.
- This framework is strongest when the goal is truth-seeking, not comfort.
