# Decision-Making Frameworks

Prompts and frameworks for making better decisions — from daily choices to major life pivots.

## Frameworks

| Framework | Source | Best For |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| [Ray Dalio's Principles](ray-dalio-principles/) | Ray Dalio | Radical transparency, idea meritocracy, systematic decision-making |

## Core Concepts

### Believability-Weighted Thinking
Not all opinions are equal. Weight advice by track record in that domain.

### Second-Order Thinking
First-order thinking: "This will save me money."
Second-order thinking: "But the quality drop will cost me more long-term."

### Pain + Reflection = Progress
Dalio's formula: Use painful decisions as data for improvement.

## When to Use These

- Major life decisions (career, relocation, relationships)
- Business pivots or investments
- When you feel stuck or biased
- When you need external perspective on emotional decisions

## Quick Prompt

**For any decision:**

```
I'm deciding between [option A] and [option B]. 

Apply Ray Dalio's framework:
1. What's the believability-weighted perspective here?
2. What are second and third-order consequences of each option?
3. What's the machine here — what cause-effect loops are driving this?
4. If we're radically transparent, what's really true that I'm avoiding?
5. What principles should govern this decision?
```
